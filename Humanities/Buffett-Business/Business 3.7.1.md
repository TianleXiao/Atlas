# Cash vs cash flow

Cash is a business's most liquid asset – it is notes and coins held in hand/in a safe as well as funds held in the business's bank accounts.
Cash flow refers to the movement of a business organization's cash inflows (cash received from the sale of goods and/or services) and cash outflows (used to pay for the costs of running the business).
Cash flow forecasting and cash flow statements are often used to measure the financial health of a business. This is because the comparison of cash inflows and cash outflows enables managers to determine whether the organization is able to pay its costs in order to maintain its business operations.
### Cash flow statement
A financial statement that aggregates a company's cash inflows and outflows from operations, investing, and financing over a set period of time.
