## Market targeting
The target market can apply to both existing and potential customers.
For a market segment to be attractive for a business to target, it must be:
- **measurable**, so the business can identify it exists and measure its size to decide on the likely earnings.
- **accessible**, so the business has the resources to offer what would be required and be able to get its products to the customers.
- **profitable**, so the business can meet customer needs and its own needs at the same time.
**Segmentation is more likely to create cost-effective targeting strategies and marketing campaigns. Alternatively, the lack of market segmentation and targeting results in a marketing mix likely to be inappropriate or ineffective for the organization's customers.**

## Market positioning
"Positioning is a revolutionary idea precisely because it cuts across the other 4Ps. It informs each of the Ps and adds consistency to them."  
- Professor Philip Kotler (b. 1931)
A product position map (sometimes referred to as a perception map) is a graphical illustration of customer perception of a business, its products or brands in comparison to other firms in the industry. The contrast is based on two pre-determined criteria, such as price and quality. It shows the perception of customers by positioning the organization, product or brand relative to the positions of rivals in the same market.

## Market positioning example
## 市场定位示例

Businesses do not compete against all other brands within the same market. For example, Honda's Jazz and NSX models cater for completely different market segments. The same applies to Nissan's Micra and GTR models. Instead, businesses tend to differentiate their products and brands to meet the needs and wants of their different target markets.

企业并不会与同一市场内的所有其他品牌展开竞争。例如，本田的飞度（Jazz）和 NSX 车型所面向的市场细分就完全不同。日产的玛驰（Micra）和 GTR 车型也是如此。相反，企业倾向于通过差异化其产品和品牌，来满足不同目标市场的需求和愿望。

Position mapping can help a business to identify potential gaps in the market. For example, both Mercedes Benz and BMW traditionally produce cars in the 'premium products' quadrant of a position map. This meant it was not catering for customers who preferred 'economy products', which led both manufacturers to produce smaller cars, such as the Mercedes A class and BMW 1 Series, to cater for a different target market.

定位图可以帮助企业识别市场中的潜在空白。例如，梅赛德斯-奔驰和宝马传统上都将汽车定位在定位图的“高端产品”象限中。这意味着它们并未满足那些偏好“经济型产品”的客户需求，这促使两家制造商纷纷推出更小型的汽车，例如奔驰 A 级和宝马 1 系，以迎合不同的目标市场。

The price of a new Porsche can range from $46,475 to over $200,000 – so it is somewhat subjective to position the brand in terms of 'price'. Similarly, a Ford Fiesta starts from around $14,115 but a Ford F-450 pick-up truck can cost in the region of $85,805.

一辆新保时捷的价格从 46,475 美元到超过 200,000 美元不等，因此仅从“价格”维度来定位该品牌具有一定的主观性。同样，福特嘉年华（Fiesta）的起售价约为 14,115 美元，但福特 F-450 皮卡的价格却可能高达 85,805 美元左右。

## Position map
## 定位图

Position maps enables marketing managers to improve their product portfolio management. Having a better picture of the way customers perceive a particular organization, product or brand helps a business to adjust its marketing mix. A reposition strategy is required if the business does not like how it is currently positioned(perceived) by customers. It can do this by creating an improved marketing mix, possibly targeting different market segments.

定位图能够帮助营销经理优化其产品组合管理。通过更清晰地了解客户对特定组织、产品或品牌的认知，企业可以更好地调整其营销组合。如果企业对其目前的市场定位（即客户的认知）不满意，则需要采取重定位策略。企业可以通过制定改进后的营销组合来实现这一目标，并可能针对不同的细分市场进行定位。
