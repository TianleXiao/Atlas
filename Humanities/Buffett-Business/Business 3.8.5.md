## The average rate of return (arr) method

- The average rate of return (**ARR**) is a method of investment appraisal that calculates the average annual profit of an investment project expressed as a percentage of the initial amount of money invested in the project.
- The average rate of return measures the forecast annual profit from a particular investment project. The **ARR** figure for any given investment project is compared to the firm's predetermined acceptable rate of return, as it helps managers to decide whether to accept or reject the proposed investment project. The average rate of return (**ARR**) is calculated by using the formula:
$$
ARR = \frac{\text{Average Profit}}{\text{Cost of the Investment}} \times 100
$$

- Where average annual profit = total profit before taxation over the asset's lifetime / useful life of the asset in years.
- And total profit before taxation = net cash flow resulting from the investment – the capital cost of the investment.
Or use the 3-in-1 formula:

$$
\frac{(\text{total returns} - \text{capital cost}) \div \text{years of use}}{\text{capital cost}} \times 100$$
