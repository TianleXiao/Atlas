## Market segmentation
Market segmentation describes the process of dividing a market for a product into smaller distinct groups of customers in an effort to meet their specific desired needs and wants. Understanding the different needs of these customer sub-groups within a market enables marketers to target their customers better. Examples of market segments include:
- **Age**
- **Cultural background**
- **Educational attainment**
- **Ethnicity**
- **Family size**
- **Gender**
- **Geographic location**
- **Income**
- **Lifestyle**
- **Occupation**
- **Religion**
- **Social status**

## Market segmentation

### Types of market segmentation
- **Geography**
  - Country
  - City
  - Density
  - Language
  - Climate
  - Area
  - Population

- **Demographics**
  - Age
  - Gender
  - Income
  - Education
  - Social status
  - Family
  - Life stage
  - Occupation

- **Psychographics**
  - Lifestyle
  - AIO: activity, interest, opinion
  - Concerns
  - Personality
  - Values
  - Attitudes

- **Behavioral**
  - Benefits/sought
  - Purchase
  - Usage
  - Intent
  - Occasion
  - Buyer stage
  - User status
  - Life cycle stage
  - Engagement
