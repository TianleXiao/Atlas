## PBP exam practice
Rowlands Printing Co. is considering an investment of $380,000 on new printing machines.  
The project is expected to generate the following net cash flows in the five years that the machines are expected to last, before they need replacing and upgrading. Rowlands Printing Co. has suffered from liquidity problems recently, although remains profitable. The management team would therefore prefer a shorter payback period for any investment project.

| Year | Net cash flow |
|------|---------------|
| 1    | $120,000      |
| 2    | $140,000      |
| 3    | $180,000      |
| 4    | $150,000      |
| 5    | $110,000      |

(a) Define the term payback period.  
[2 marks]
(b) Calculate the PBP for Rowlands Printing Co.  
[2 marks]
(c) Comment on whether Rowlands Printing Co. should invest in the new printing machines.  
[4 marks]

## Comparing investment returns

Two investment projects, A and B, each require an investment of $1 million.  
Their expected earnings are shown in the table below.  
Which investment project is more attractive?

| Year | Project A cash inflow ($) | Project B cash inflow ($) |
|------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| 1    | 500,000                   | 100,000                   |
| 2    | 300,000                   | 100,000                   |
| 3    | 100,000                   | 300,000                   |
| 4    | 100,000                   | 500,000                   |

- ✅ Both investment projects achieve payback at the end of Year 4.
- ✅ Project A is obviously more attractive because it yields greater returns in the early years.

## The pbp method benefits
- It is the easiest and fastest method to appraise investment. The results are also easy to understand and interpret/comment.
- It is useful for businesses that suffer from cash flow problems, such as small sole traders, or small businesses trying to survive a recession.
- It is also suitable for businesses in fast-changing industries, where products and trends can become outdated quite quickly.
- It aids decision making, as managers will tend to choose investments with a shorter PBP in order to reduce risks and avoid cash flow issues.
